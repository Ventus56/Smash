Eating a healthy, balanced diet is an important part of maintaining good health, but sometimes that might not be the case. Food borne diseases are diseases that targets the digestive system.

What are food borne diseases?

Food borne diseases are diseases that are located in edible sources that we call food. Food borne diseases can be a pretty scary thing if it goes too far and become very fatal but luckily for us most food borne diseases aren't that fatal to us, but food borne diseases can really harm businesses that depends on selling that type of product.

What is salmonella?

Salmonella was first discovered in 1885 by a scientist by the name of Daniel. E Salmon. Salmonella has been around for a long time but no one actually saw what it looks like microscopically till 1880.

How can salmonella be treated?

In some conditions of salmonella can go away if the person is healthy it can go away with self care in 1 to 2 days. Antibiotics can also stop the growth of the bacteria. Some other cases Salmonella can be fatal and in fact about 450 out 1.2 million people die a year from salmonella.

Who is at risk?

America is probably the most common place for salmonella since it has about 1.2 million of Salmonella cases happened. Most of the Salmonella are in raw meats such as beef, poultry and sea food.
